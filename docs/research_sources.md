# Trusted Research Sources

These curated, attributed sources form the evidence base for public claims on the
Mended Health IT AI Advisory site. Every statistic used in marketing copy must
trace back to one of these. No fabricated data, logos, testimonials, or
partnerships are permitted.

Sources are organized by the themes used on the Research and Trust page:
Adoption, Governance, Clinical safety, Workforce transformation, Revenue cycle
automation, Legal and regulatory, and Market context.

---

## Adoption

### JAMA Network Open / PMC: Uptake of Generative AI Integrated With EHRs in US Hospitals
- Survey of 2,174 nonfederal US hospitals.
- 31.5% reported using generative AI in 2024.
- 24.7% planned to use generative AI within one year.
- More than half of US hospitals likely implement generative AI by end of 2025.
- Some hospitals are adopting without complete safeguards.

**Use:** Supports urgency and readiness assessment positioning.

## Governance

### Stanford HAI / JAMA Summit Report
- AI is transforming health and healthcare rapidly.
- There is little consensus or structure for evaluation, regulation,
  implementation, and monitoring.
- Alignment is needed between developers, health systems, payers, regulators,
  and patients.

**Use:** Supports governance, monitoring, and independent advisory need.

## Clinical safety

### Wolters Kluwer: Future Ready Healthcare 2026
- 52% of patients use AI to research health conditions.
- 74% of patients trust AI-generated answers but expect doctors to validate them.
- 77% of clinicians validate AI-generated health information because of bias,
  hallucination, and misinformation risk.
- 92% of doctors and 90% of nurses believe clinical AI outputs should be
  validated by a human expert-in-the-loop.
- 90% of physicians and nurses identify efficiency-enhancing technology as a top
  trend.

**Use:** Supports trust, validation, clinical-grade AI, and human oversight
positioning.

## Workforce transformation

### SullivanCotter: AI Workforce Transformation
- AI will produce first drafts of notes, summaries, and orders.
- Clinicians will focus more on validation, interpretation, and decision-making.
- Administrative functions will see faster ROI because work is rules-based and
  measurable.
- AI literacy and responsible-use principles should become formal workforce
  capabilities.

**Use:** Supports workforce planning and AI literacy offer.

## Legal and regulatory

### University of Illinois College of Law / NEJM discussion of the Utah AI Prescription Renewal Pilot
- Utah launched a 12-month pilot in January 2026 allowing AI to independently
  renew certain prescriptions.
- Applies to nearly 200 chronic-condition medications.
- Raises legal and ethical issues around FDA authorization, misbranding, and
  autonomous prescribing.

**Use:** Supports regulatory risk tracking and legal review positioning.
